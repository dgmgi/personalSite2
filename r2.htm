R2: Introduction to Design Thinking Process
Design Thinking at the start is all about finding the problem and closing down on it, which is what I’m not used to. Usually when I’m in a group, they always immediately try to find a solution, and this is what I did to for a very long time. Then came the Design Process.

I find the Design Process a bit hard to understand. There are three “phases”, Assessing, Designing, and Building. Assessing is where you “Understand the Problem” and “Generate Possible Solutions”. What I found confusing is that, why would you generate possible solutions in the Assessing phase. I asked myself, “Shouldn’t that be in the Design Phase?”.

Design Phase is Analyzing and Selecting from a list of proposed solutions. What I find hard to understand here is if you are going to do some prototyping, or just select a solution, plain and simple. This is when I started understanding a little bit as to why but can’t seem to put it into words. The way I see it, Assessing is some sort of Ideation, while in Designing Phase, you finalize the proposed solution after assessment. Finally, we have to Build the project. I think this is the true prototyping phase.

What I like about the Design Thinking Process is you don’t go in a problem blindly. From the start, you assess many things that can really help you in formulating a possible solution such as knowing your target audience and putting yourself in their shoes, and see how it affects them.
